## 3. §34.9 Addendum Protocol (text to add to the Constitution)

> **34.9 Addendum protocol.** Every cycle after the kernel cycle proceeds as follows, and no cycle's delta plan may be authored before the previous cycle's product-integrity close passes.
>
> 1. **Archaeology.** The delta plan opens with a repository-reality survey (code, tests, contracts, drift-audit output). Claims about existing structure cite the survey, never a prior plan's description.
> 2. **Kernel-touch declaration.** The delta plan declares `kernel-touch: none` or enumerates the touched kernel laws. Any touch requires: kernel version bump, migration plan for all affected identities/receipts, claim-lapse review, and Charter change-control entry.
> 3. **Contracts before beads.** Every new capability receives a consequence contract citing its SpecCardIds; falsifiers become bead acceptance criteria; the assigned Gauntlet tier is the definition of done.
> 4. **Delta-only conversion.** Beads are generated for the delta plan only. Full-plan reconversion is prohibited (feature-loss and drift vector). Polish to steady state; dependency graph acyclic.
> 5. **Execution.**
> 6. **Product/evaluation-integrity close (blocking for cycle progression or package submission):** (a) the cycle-specific functional and security gate; (b) drift audit—generated-truth diffs, contract-truth checks, claim-registry consistency, and zero findings classified `A` under the then-governing review protocol, whose grade namespace is recorded with the result; historical Audit-v1 letters are not reinterpreted; (c) SpecCard coverage check for the cycle's surface; and (d) a cycle record conforming to the current `CycleTrialRecordSchemaId`. This close requires no external steward and confers no §33 credit by itself.
> 7. **§33 evidence-admission extension (blocking only for use as Q2/Q3/§33 evidence):** (a) one independently stewarded, budget-respecting held-out extension probe, recorded win-or-lose; (b) independent seal/time commitment of the cycle record; and (c) every schema disclosure required for the intended claim. If this extension is unavailable, the cycle may ship after its product-integrity close, but the miss is logged, the record is excluded from claim evidence, and the affected claim downgrades rather than receiving silent credit.
>
> **One normative record interface.** The Constitution assigns a versioned `CycleTrialRecordSchemaId` as the sole normative home for cycle-record fields. It records: cycle and record type; branch/attempt relationships; attempts and success rate; median/percentiles and any declared selection or extremum rule; retries/restarts; stopping/peeking and branch-retention policy; model, provider, harness, tool, and routing observations plus uncertainty; wall time, compute/cost, and human interventions; defect distribution; representative failures; discarded or abandoned branches; evaluator-guided repairs; drift-audit and probe outcomes; held-out seal/query-budget state; and privacy, redaction, retention, and unavailable-field limits. Charter and Reasoning summaries cite this ID and do not redefine shorter schemas.
>
> Records #1–#6 are construction-cycle records. Record #7 is an evaluation-cycle record: it preserves evaluation attempts, failures, interventions, and lineage but is never pooled as candidate construction. The foundry production-attempt distribution consumes only evidence-admitted construction records. A cycle record without the evidence-admission extension remains operational lineage and contributes nothing to a §33 claim regardless of what the cycle shipped.
