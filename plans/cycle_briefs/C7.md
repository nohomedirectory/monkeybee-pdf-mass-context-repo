## 2. The three-commit law governs everything in this cycle

§24.16 makes field measurement three irreversible, ordered stages. C7 is the third. It cannot repair the first two.

1. **Pre-search discovery commitment** — field and capability definitions, eligibility, search surfaces, terms, languages, scouts, cutoff law, nomination and challenge procedure, planned exclusions. Committed **before searching**. (Day zero.)
2. **Discovery execution and field freeze** — scouts execute, deviations are recorded, candidates are characterized, the nomination and challenge window *runs*, and a `CompetitorDiscoveryReportId` freezes the comparator set. (Unscheduled — matrix F-4.)
3. **Pre-measurement evaluation commitment** — an `EvaluationProtocolCommitment` binds that exact frozen report, the comparator configurations and symmetric tuning budgets, corpus, metrics, statistics, stopping rule, adjudication, and claim lifecycle — **before measurement or result inspection**. (C7.)

§33.4 forecloses the shortcut in one sentence: "one late omnibus document cannot retroactively satisfy all three boundaries." And §24.16 states the penalty for skipping: "If the `FieldDefinitionId` is absent/ambiguous, discovery coverage is incomplete, or its protocol was not prospectively committed, **only `named_set_lead` is permitted**" — a result over exactly the artifacts you happened to name, which is not a field lead and not an answer to Q2 as asked.

## 6. Known risks

1. **Retroactive protocol authorship.** The strongest pressure in this cycle is to write the measurement protocol after glimpsing a result. §24.12 rule 6 and §33.4 both forbid it, and the resulting claim is void rather than weak. The antidote is that the protocol is content-addressed and independently time-committed before measurement — which requires the substrate that day zero was supposed to establish.
2. **Threshold and eligibility drift after seeing failures.** §24.15.1: "never relax thresholds or challenger eligibility after seeing failures." A challenger excluded because it beat us is fraud, not methodology.
3. **Metric laundering.** §24.12 rule 12: atomic scorecards, margins, Pareto relations, or a justified utility rule. Unresolved tradeoffs are `incomparable`. Unsupported, partial, timeout, resource, indeterminate, and disqualified cases stay visible. No aggregate hides a trailing safety, correctness, coverage, or resource axis.
4. **Claim lapse after submission (matrix F-9).** §24.16: a newly discovered material released challenger lapses current-facing field leads until characterized. §33.2: a contemporaneous claim can lapse while the historical one stands. The campaign ends at submission and the verdict arrives later, by design — so either both claims are declared explicitly *historical and dated at submission*, or someone owns the refresh. A lapsed claim that nobody lapses is a false claim.
5. **The publication temptation.** Every result in this cycle is publishable whether or not it flatters the project. §31.8 says so for the wild-tail ledger; §24.15.2's fallback line says "none for publication honesty; the supremacy outcome may be trailing." The vocabulary law (AGENTS rule 13) holds in the C7 package exactly as it holds everywhere else: `leading`, `equivalent`, `non_inferior`, `trailing`, `incomparable`, `indeterminate`, `disqualified` — and only an uncertainty-aware `leading` result under the exact declared field scope supports a headline.
6. **External receipts have no campaign custodian.** The Q3 review may still be pending when C7 emits the §33.16 bundle, and Q2 adjudication begins only after submission. A pending marker preserves honesty but does not assign the later update. Matrix Q3-BND-3 and Q2-IND-1 keep both handoffs visible; this brief does not route them.
