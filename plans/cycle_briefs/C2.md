# Cycle 2 brief — Render engine minus real text

**Layers:** L3 + the raster subset of L4 · **Envelope:** none ships (R1 lands at C3) · **Kernel-touch expectation:** `none`

This is a brief, not a delta plan. The C2 delta plan is authored at the C1→C2 boundary, **after** the C1 close gate passes, and it opens with an archaeology survey of the repository as C1 actually left it (Charter §6 cl. 1; Work Order §3 cl. 1). Nothing here may be read as a description of code. Where this brief says "the C2 plan will need," it is naming an obligation, not predicting an implementation.

---

## 1. Scope boundary

**In.** The content-stream lexer and the page program as a program (§17.1–17.2). The page-program VM with graphics state, the operator catalog and admission rules, scoped resource resolution, and the bounded Type 4 calculator function VM (§17.11–17.12). Paths, clipping, winding rules, and transforms (§18.1–18.7). The rasterizer and the appearance-level path pipeline — flattening, stroke expansion, coverage, clip masks (§18.3–18.7). Baseline images in device color spaces only: DCT/JPEG and Flate-backed samples (§19.9–19.11). The transparency subset that R1's declared profile actually promises (§18.8–18.9). Optional content groups and visibility traces (§17.10). `RenderReport`s carrying D2 same-build pixel determinism (§11.9, §26.10). Benchmark tracking, beginning here and running every cycle (Charter §5/C2).

**Out.** Real text. Fonts of every family, CMaps, and glyph execution are C3 — C2 ships `mb-font-protocol`'s format-neutral request/result surface and nothing behind it (§30.5). ICC and calibrated color are C3; C2 stays in device spaces (§19.3). JPX, JBIG2, and CCITT are C6 (§15.6–15.7). Shading types 4–7, full transparency (isolation, knockout, every blend mode), Separation/DeviceN, and rendering intents are C6. Any writer is C4. The GPU path stays outside every envelope until open decision 7 is resolved at C6 (§35; Work Order §4).

**Why this boundary.** Text is the single largest source of render infidelity, and a rendering stack that fakes it produces evidence nobody can trust. C2 therefore ships a render engine that is *honest about not having text*: glyphs appear as explicit substitution-or-refusal receipts under the page-local refusal law (Charter §5/C2), which is embarrassing in a screenshot and correct in an evidence package. Splitting the raster core from the text stack also gives C3 a working rasterizer to test fonts against, instead of debugging two new subsystems through each other.

## 2. Expected consequence-contract families

Contract identifiers are authored in the C2 delta plan under §10.10, never here. The families the plan must cover:

- **Content-program lexing and preservation** — tokenization, inline-image parsing, program preservation for source-preserving edits later.
- **Page-program execution** — metered VM over injected resource and font protocols; per-operator admission; compatibility sections (§17.8); marked-content linkage (§17.9).
- **Graphics state** — the persistent stack and state-transition semantics, including the current-path-outside-`q`/`Q` rule that round-1 verification already confirmed against the standard.
- **Function evaluation** — PDF function types and the bounded calculator VM, with its own budget dimension.
- **Path and raster** — construction, flattening, fill/stroke rasterization, clipping, winding.
- **Baseline image decode** — DCT and Flate-backed samples, decode arrays, image masks, provenance from pixel back to source bytes.
- **Transparency subset** — only what R1's profile declares; anything beyond it refuses page-locally rather than approximating.
- **Render reporting** — `RenderReport` identity, D2 determinism class, refusal and substitution receipts.
- **Optional content** — visibility traces as evidence, not as a silent filter.

Each family carries falsifiers per §27.1: analytic oracles for path geometry and blend algebra, filter test vectors for the codecs, cross-run and cross-thread hash checks for D2.

**The benchmark boundary — read this before writing a single benchmark.** Charter §5/C2 says "benchmark tracking begins here." That means MonkeyBee against MonkeyBee's own prior builds, stratified by §26.2's workload classes. It does **not** mean measurement against any other PDF library. AGENTS rule 9 and OVERNIGHT_GOAL §1.4 are unconditional: no measurement of or comparison against other PDF processors before the discovery protocols are committed through the tamper-evident substrate, because premature measurement permanently poisons the Q2 evidence chain. §26.3's comparative baseline lab and lane S6 are C7 activities under a committed evaluation protocol (§24.16 stage 3). A well-meaning C2 implementer who benchmarks against an incumbent to "get a sense of where we are" destroys a question this campaign exists to answer, and the damage is not reversible.

## 5. Known risks

1. **D2 determinism is easy to promise and easy to lose.** Thread-count invariance, floating-point paths, and tile scheduling all leak nondeterminism. §11.9 makes the class a per-operation declaration; the C6 perf slice must *preserve* what C2 establishes, which means C2's determinism tests are the thing C6 optimizes against. Weak tests here become an unprovable D2 claim there.
2. **Rasterizer DoS surface.** Pathological paths, huge dimensions, deep recursion, and expansion bombs (§27/G5, §32) all land in this cycle. The budget dimensions from §11.2 must cover raster work, not just parse work.
3. **Codec trust tiers.** DCT arrives via a dependency (§15.9.1). Fault isolation and the trust-tier classification are contract obligations, not a later hardening pass.
4. **The transparency subset will be under-specified until R1's profile is written.** C2 implements "the subset R1 declares" — and R1's claim envelope is published at C3. The C2 delta plan must pin the profile *before* implementation, or the subset becomes whatever the code happened to do.
5. **Text-refusal receipts are a product surface, not a placeholder.** They are what makes C2 honest. Treating them as scaffolding to be deleted at C3 loses the page-local refusal accounting that R1's envelope depends on.

## 6. Close-gate sketch

Blocking, per Charter §6 cl. 6 and Work Order §3 cl. 6, plus C2's specifics:

- **G2 render lanes green on the non-text corpus slice** (analytic and independently implemented oracles — §27/G2: exact path geometry, known blend equations, filter test vectors).
- **D2 receipts reproducible** across runs, threads, and the declared target.
- **Zero reachable panics** under the C2 fuzz targets (content lexer, operator admission, function VM, image decode, path/raster) — the C1 gate's law, extended to the new surface.
- **SpecCard coverage** for the C2 surface — cards run one cycle ahead of implementation (Charter §3 item 5), so the C2 cards are produced during C1.
- **Drift audit** — generated-truth diffs, contract-truth checks, claim-registry consistency; zero Grade-A findings.
- **One held-out extension probe** from the sealed pool, recorded win or lose.
- **Sealed cycle trial record #2** into the production ledger.
- **Kernel-touch declaration:** `none` expected. Any touch triggers a kernel version bump, a migration plan for every affected identity and receipt, a claim-lapse review, and a Charter change-control entry (Work Order §3 cl. 2). The campaign's design target is zero kernel-touch from C2 onward; this is the first cycle where that target is tested.
