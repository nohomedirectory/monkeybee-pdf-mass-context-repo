# Cycle 5 brief — Transforms and security; R3 ships; the CDR wedge reaches its Charter endpoint

**Layers:** L5–L7 · **Envelope:** **R3 — Transformation and Security ships** · **Kernel-touch expectation:** `none`

This cycle carries the commercial wedge to the endpoint Charter §2 sets for it. Goal G1 — a sellable secure-ingestion product, live after C1 and improving every cycle — reaches the content-disarm-and-reconstruction deliverable boundary that Charter §5/C5 defines. What that boundary admits and excludes is fixed by the R3 envelope (§3.0) and by each capability's own no-claim boundary; this brief asserts no coverage beyond them. Everything in this cycle must be able to stand alone if the campaign's evidence arm is cut tomorrow, because that is precisely what the graceful-degradation law promises.

---

## 1. Scope boundary

**In.** The remaining decryption matrix: the public-key security handlers that C1's standard-handler read path left open, under an explicit key-provider profile (§16.2.1; audit ledger C-1). Canonical rewrite with carry/drop maps (§23.4). Sanitization: versioned active-content, attachment, form, metadata, and history policies (§23.12, §16.9). **Redaction with the inference-resistance battery and Flagship D** (§23.8–23.10, §31.4). Layered **cryptographic** signature validation — CMS structure and primitive verification, DSS/VRI, timestamps — with trust stores, clocks, and revocation kept as explicit host adapters (§16.6–16.7). The secure-output profile: D1R re-encryption and the nonce law (§11.9, §16.1.1). Semantic diff (§23.5). The EditSession and agent surface, with idempotency and stale-state protection (§23.1–23.2, §25.9). The **C ABI preview** (Workflow M, §25.4) — a preview, explicitly before any compatibility commitment.

**Out.** Signature *creation* — a stated non-goal (§3.5), and Bet 23's evidence envelopes are not it. The C-ABI *freeze* is post-R4 (Work Order §4). Advanced codecs, advanced ICC, full transparency, forms appearance generation, and the authoring track are C6. The coverage-matrix scorer is C6.

**Why this boundary.** Redaction, sanitization, and signature validation are the three places where a PDF tool's claim is load-bearing for a user's safety, and each of them fails in a way the user cannot see. That is why they arrive together, behind the whole read stack, with independent falsifiers attached — and why the *cryptographic* half of signature validation waits for a cycle whose entire subject is security rather than riding along with C4's writer.

## 2. Expected consequence-contract families

- **Public-key decryption** — under an explicit key-provider capability; the key never enters an ordinary log (§27/G6.1 taint fixtures).
- **Canonical rewrite** — carry and drop maps as first-class outputs: what survived, what did not, and why.
- **Sanitization** — one contract per policy class, versioned; the executable-feature registry shared with any admitted execution path (§30.7 `mb-action`).
- **Redaction** — intent model, content surgery, full-rewrite removal, negative-evidence checks, and a **separately scoped** inference-resistance assessment. The negative-evidence claim is scoped to the enumerated search and transformation mechanisms — never to "the information is gone."
- **Cryptographic signature validation** — integrity, authentication, trust, role authorization, policy admission, and freshness as **separate outcomes** (§35), never one green check.
- **Secure output** — D1R: the admitted semantic plan and non-secret receipt are reproducible; byte identity is *not* promised where entropy or external inputs enter (§11.9).
- **Semantic diff** — byte, object, program, appearance, glyph, text, structure, and security axes.
- **Edit sessions and the agent surface** — propose→validate→apply; stale-root and digest validation; least-authority leases; idempotency with an explicit `indeterminate` outcome (§35: no global exactly-once claim).
- **C ABI preview** — preview semantics stated as preview; no compatibility promise.

## 3. Flagship D is this cycle's evidence centerpiece

§31.4 requires the redaction output to survive: raw and decoded byte searches; glyph, text, and structure searches; an optional OCR adapter on images; an independent forensic tool; **and an independently sourced forensic path whose shared parser, model, or data lineage is disclosed.** Audit finding R1-3 flagged those last two bullets as near-duplicates — either merge residue or an unstated two-tool lineage-diversity requirement. The Charter's C5 gate resolves it in the second direction: "Flagship D passes with **disclosed tool lineage**." Two forensic tools that share a parser are one oracle wearing two hats (§10.2.1), and a redaction claim grounded that way is grounded on correlation.

The honest boundary is written into §31.4 itself: reliable semantic targeting inside images and badly encoded fonts is what breaks first. The system preserves that uncertainty rather than silently leaving or over-removing content — and Rev 7 §3.0's R3 row already forbids the absolute claim that secrets hidden in arbitrary vector art can be detected automatically.

## 6. Known risks

1. **The redaction claim is the easiest claim in the project to overstate.** "Removed" is a scoped statement about enumerated mechanisms, not a metaphysical one. §23.10's negative evidence exists to keep it scoped, and §31.4's honesty clause exists because the temptation is real.
2. **Signature validation conflates six outcomes into one.** Integrity, authentication, trust, role authorization, policy admission, and freshness are separate (§35), and every viewer in the world collapses them into a green checkmark. MonkeyBee's contract must not.
3. **Nonce reuse.** Silent, catastrophic, and unrecoverable. The nonce law and its allocator fault tests are contract obligations, not hardening.
4. **The agent surface is an injection surface.** Prompt-injection payloads, hostile proposals, and stale-state adversaries belong in the fuzz inventory (§27/G5), and least-authority leases are what keep a compromised proposal from becoming a compromised document.
5. **Sanitizer/interpreter parity has a dependent that does not exist.** §31.7's Flagship G requires parity to be *proved* over the shared executable-feature registry that `mb-action` owns. Flagship G's own capability surface is scheduled by no cycle (matrix F-1). C5 should still build `mb-action`'s registry properly — sanitization needs it regardless — but the C5 plan must not assume a consumer that the Charter never schedules.
