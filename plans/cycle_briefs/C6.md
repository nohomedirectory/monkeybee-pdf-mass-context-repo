# Cycle 6 brief — Advanced fidelity, authoring, performance, and the coverage scorer; R4 ships

**Layers:** L4–L7 · **Envelope:** **R4 — Advanced Profiles and Rare Features ships** · **Kernel-touch expectation:** `none`

This is the most loaded cycle in the map: two capability tracks, a performance-hardening slice, and the measurement instrument that Q2 depends on. The brief says so plainly rather than pretending the load is ordinary.

---

## 1. Scope boundary

**Track A — fidelity.** JPX, JBIG2, and CCITT land here (§15.4–15.7). Separation/DeviceN and rendering intents (§19.7–19.8). Full transparency: isolation, knockout, and every blend mode (§18.8–18.9). Shading types 4–7 (§18.12). Tagged-PDF structure semantics (§21.1–21.3). The AcroForm model and appearance generation (§21.7–21.8). PDF/A and PDF/UA validation families (§24.2, §30.9). Portfolios and the preserve-and-inventory tail.

**Track B — authoring.** The structured-authoring layout engine: UAX #14 line breaking, UAX #29 segmentation, UAX #9 bidi, shaping ownership per Bet 17 (§22.9). Tagged-output authoring, so PDF/UA-conformant *generation* exists and not only PDF/UA *validation* (§22.12).

**Plus.** The **performance-hardening slice**: parallel page rendering, arena and structure-of-arrays layouts, with D2 determinism preserved under optimization (§26.5–26.10). The **coverage-matrix scorer**, built over the clause tagging that has existed since C1 (Charter §5/C6).

**Out.** Any admitted execution of form actions. C6 ships the AcroForm *model* and *appearance generation* — and §30.8 defines `mb-form-appearance` as generating appearances "without executing unsupported dynamic logic." Bet 16's metered calculate/format/validate subset and bounded static-XFA projection are [M], capability-gated, and non-blocking for every declared envelope R0–R4 (§3.5). They are not in this cycle, and no other cycle claims them — see §5 below, because a flagship at C7 depends on them.

**Why this boundary.** R4 is the "broad PDF civilization coverage" envelope, and its two tracks are genuinely separable: reading rare features and authoring accessible documents share a semantic core (§P11) but almost no failure modes. They land together because R4 is a single claim envelope, and because Track B's layout engine is the last major subsystem the coverage matrix needs before it can score authoring clauses at all.

## 2. Expected consequence-contract families

- **Advanced codecs** — one contract per codec, each with an elevated security gate. §15.9.1's trust tiers and fault isolation are the design premise, not a mitigation.
- **Advanced color** — Separation/DeviceN, colorant-aware values, rendering intents, with device-dependent controls stated as device-dependent.
- **Full transparency** — isolation, knockout, and every blend mode the R4 profile admits; D2 preserved.
- **Shading types 4–7** — mesh evaluation, with analytic oracles.
- **Tagged structure semantics** — the structure tree, parent tree, namespaces, attributes, and the structure-to-appearance linkage.
- **AcroForm model and appearance generation** — fields, values, widgets, existing-appearance selection, policy-defined appearance writing. No dynamic execution.
- **Validation profiles** — PDF/A and PDF/UA families as versioned programs (§24.1), with independent validators (§24.3) rather than self-checking.
- **Layout and authoring** — line breaking, segmentation, bidi, shaping; tagged output generation with structure receipts.
- **Performance** — the D-class contracts from C1 made real; benchmark rows tied to consequence contracts (§27.4), never free-floating numbers.
- **Coverage scorer** — reads clause tagging; never writes it.

## 5. Two structural problems this cycle inherits, and cannot fix alone

**The scorer measures one side of a two-sided matrix (matrix F-3).** C6's scorer reads MonkeyBee's clause tagging and produces MonkeyBee's clause coverage. Q2 asks for a coverage matrix "across MonkeyBee **and the field**" (Charter §5/C7). Scoring a competitor's clause coverage requires driving it as a black-box subject over per-clause fixtures under §4.6 — an instrument no cycle builds — and §24.12 rule 4 forbids the alternative outright: "Documentation silence is not absence." C6 should build the scorer it was asked for and should not silently widen it into a field instrument it was not scoped, staffed, or gated to build. The gap belongs to the human and to C0's day-zero commitments, not to C6's swarm.

**The performance slice is a conditional prerequisite for lanes nobody executes (matrix F-2).** Charter §5/C6 calls this slice "the S6/S1 prerequisite." S1 (rendering field lead) and S6 (performance escalation) are §24.15 lanes, and no cycle in the map executes them — C7 names only Flagships G and H. That does not make the slice a reverse orphan: Charter §2 requires the G1 wedge to improve every cycle, and §5/C6 independently authorizes performance hardening as product work. It does mean the slice cannot be credited to Q3-SUB-1 or treated as if an S1/S6 comparison follows. C6 should **not** adopt a competitive posture, benchmark against other processors, or produce comparative numbers: §35 open decision 9 keeps S6 inactive while §26.3's non-competitive baseline holds, AGENTS rule 9 forbids the measurement outright until the discovery protocols are committed, and lane execution — if it happens at all — is C7's under a committed evaluation protocol.

## 6. Known risks

1. **Cycle overload.** Two capability tracks, a performance slice, and an instrument. If C1 telemetry showed the polish rounds straining, this is the cycle where a compressed variant (Charter §9) would have been the wrong call. The delta plan should state, explicitly, what it will drop first if capacity runs out — and the answer should be Track A's rare tail, never the scorer, because the scorer is Q2's instrument and the tail is R4's long grass.
2. **Codec security.** JBIG2 and JPX are historically the highest-severity attack surface in the entire format. Elevated gate, fault isolation, fuzzing from hour one.
3. **D2 determinism versus optimization.** Parallel page rendering and arena layouts are exactly the changes that break bit-identical raster output. §26.10 makes D2 the required default; the C2 determinism tests are what stand between "optimized" and "quietly nondeterministic."
4. **Accessibility claims outrun accessibility evidence.** A PDF/UA validator pass is one subclaim; it is not usability and not authorial intent (§31.10's honesty clause). Tagged *generation* must carry structure receipts, and inferred structure that cannot be defended must refuse.
5. **The AcroForm model invites the form-action surface.** It is adjacent, it is tempting, and it is [M]-tagged, capability-gated, and outside R0–R4 (§3.5). Building it here without a Charter change-control entry would put a moonshot on the release critical path, which §P18 forbids.
